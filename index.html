Digital Sovereignty.

Advocating for explicit user consent in the evolution of artificial intelligence and data processing.

The Mandate

In a society defined by automated systems, the protection of individual autonomy is paramount. This legislation is rooted in one foundational principle: AI should not access personal information that is not explicitly given by a user.

We believe innovation flourishes best when built on a foundation of trust and verifiable consent.

The Framework

Surveillance

Establishing boundaries against mass facial recognition and unauthorized biometric tracking.

Confidentiality

Preventing automated systems from indexing private communications for corporate or state use.

Consent

Requiring all AI models to provide proof of affirmative, voluntary opt-in before data ingestion.

The Efficiency Argument

Data integrity increases when information is provided voluntarily. By focusing on explicit consent, we ensure that AI used in critical sectors like medicine and security is more accurate and reliable.
