AI Privacy Regulations

Ensuring "Explicit Consent" in the Age of Automation.

The Factual Problem: Data Scraping & Monitoring

Modern AI systems are often trained on "Dark Data"—information scraped from the web without user knowledge or consent. According to recent 2025 privacy reports, AI-driven workplace monitoring and biometric inferences (tracking your voice and behavior) have become standard, often bypassing traditional privacy settings.

Our law solves this by mandating that AI should not access personal information that is not explicitly given by a user.

The Three Pillars of the Regulation

1. Data Minimization

AI models must only process the specific data required for the task. No "bulk" collection of citizen data for unspecified future use.

2. Anti-Surveillance

Strictly prohibits AI from being used for mass public surveillance, facial recognition tracking in private spaces, or state-level espionage.

3. The Right to Opt-In

Aligning with the 2026 EU AI Act standards, users must provide affirmative, explicit consent before their data is used for AI training.

Addressing the Innovation Myth

Criticism: "We need AI to manage private info for hospitals and anti-terrorism to stay competitive."

The Factual Reality: This law does not ban AI; it mandates Privacy by Design. In fact, Pew Research (2025) shows that 80% of citizens are more likely to use AI tools if they know their data is protected. By ensuring consent, we build trust, which actually accelerates the adoption of AI in healthcare and public safety without turning into a dystopian reality like Orwell's 1984.

Why Voters Stand With Us

Privacy is a fundamental right. Without regulation, we risk a world where every movement, purchase, and private conversation is "learned" by an algorithm for the benefit of a few corporations or government entities.
