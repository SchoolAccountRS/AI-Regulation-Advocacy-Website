AI Privacy Regulations

Protecting the right to explicit consent in an automated world.

The Case for Data Sovereignty

Modern AI systems often process "unsolicited data streams"—personal information collected without a user's knowledge. This law establishes a simple, ethical boundary: **AI should not access personal information that is not explicitly given by a user.**

The Regulatory Framework

Anti-Surveillance

Prevents AI from being used for mass surveillance or the unauthorized tracking of citizens in public spaces.

No Domestic Espionage

Strictly prohibits AI from serving as a tool to uncover or collect private information on citizens for state or corporate use.

Explicit Opt-In

Shifts the legal burden to the processor, requiring verifiable consent before any data is used for training or analysis.

Addressing Innovation Concerns

"Does this hinder the use of AI in healthcare or public safety?"

Professional Analysis: No. This law focuses on unsolicited access. Public institutions can continue to leverage AI for efficiency, provided the data utilized is voluntarily submitted for those specific purposes. Protecting consent builds the public trust required for long-term technological adoption.

Why This Matters Now

Privacy is the foundation of a free society. Without these regulations, we risk establishing systems of automated governance that lack human oversight—a digital parallel to the surveillance risks historically noted in mid-20th-century literature. We must ensure that humans, not algorithms, remain in control of our futures.
