The Future of Digital Autonomy.

Ensuring explicit consent remains the standard for all automated data processing and AI integration.

The Landscape of Modern Data

In an increasingly automated environment, the boundary between public and private information has blurred. This regulation codifies a necessary standard: AI should not access personal information that is not explicitly given by a user.

By addressing unsolicited data processing at the source, we protect citizens from non-consensual profiling while fostering a climate of ethical innovation.

The Regulatory Framework

Integrity Standards

Prevents AI applications from serving as tools for domestic espionage or the unsolicited gathering of private communications.

Surveillance Oversight

Restricts the deployment of persistent AI-driven mass surveillance and unauthorized biometric tracking in public squares.

Transparency Mandates

Requires verifiable, affirmative consent before any personal data is utilized for model training or behavioral analysis.

A Perspective on Public Safety:

Critics suggest that privacy standards may hinder critical services. However, professional data analysis indicates that explicitly provided data is higher in quality and reliability. By focusing on voluntarily submitted information, public institutions can leverage AI with higher precision and greater public trust.

Stand for Digital Sovereignty

Protecting explicit consent ensures that citizens, not algorithms, remain the architects of their digital footprint. Join the movement to secure a balanced, private future.
