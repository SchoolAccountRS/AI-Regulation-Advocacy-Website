AI Privacy Regulations

Your data belongs to you—not the algorithm.

Join the Movement

The Problem: Non-Consensual Access

AI should not access personal information that is not explicitly given by a user. Currently, AI systems scrape data without permission, turning your private life into training data for corporations.

Core Regulations

No Surveillance

Stops AI from being used for mass surveillance of the general public.

No Espionage

Prevents AI from being used as a tool for spying or tracking citizens' private lives.

Explicit Consent

Ensures information is only collected when a user specifically opts-in.

Who Benefits?

Every Citizen. This law protects individuals who value their privacy and want to maintain control over their digital footprint in an increasingly automated world.

Addressing Criticisms

"Won't this stop AI innovation in hospitals or law enforcement?"

The Truth: No. The law says AI should not access info not explicitly given. It does not stop AI from managing data provided by a hospital or agency; it simply ensures that the data is obtained ethically. Privacy is the foundation of innovation, not an obstacle to it.

Why Your Vote Matters:

Without these regulations, we risk sliding into a dystopian reality—a world like Orwell's 1984 where your every move is micromanaged by an invisible eye. Support this law to keep our future free, private, and human-centric.
