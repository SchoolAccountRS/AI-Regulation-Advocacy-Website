The Digital Mandate

As artificial intelligence becomes increasingly integrated into the social fabric, the preservation of individual autonomy is paramount. Our proposal is built on a singular, clean principle: AI should not access personal information that is not explicitly given by a user.

By enforcing this standard, we move away from "presumed consent" and toward a future where every interaction is intentional and verified.

The Framework

Surveillance

Establishing clear prohibitions against AI-driven mass surveillance and the unauthorized collection of public biometric data.

Espionage

Preventing the use of automated systems as tools for domestic espionage or the unsolicited tracking of private communications.

Consent

Moving the legal burden to data processors to secure affirmative, verifiable consent before any citizen data is utilized.

Integrity

Ensuring that the growth of AI technology does not outpace the fundamental rights of the individuals it aims to serve.

A Note on Public Interest

Professional assessment indicates that privacy regulations do not hinder public safety; they improve it. By ensuring that AI only processes data that is explicitly provided, we increase the accuracy of systems in healthcare and law enforcement while maintaining the public trust necessary for innovation.
