Formalizing the requirement for explicit user consent in the evolution of artificial intelligence.

The Mandate

In a society defined by automated processing, the protection of individual autonomy is the defining challenge of our era. This legislation is rooted in one foundational principle: AI should not access personal information that is not explicitly given by a user.

We believe that innovation flourishes best in an environment of trust. By eliminating "silent data collection," we return the keys of the digital world to the citizens who inhabit it.

The Framework

Surveillance

Establishing federal boundaries against mass facial recognition and biometric data collection in public spaces.

Confidentiality

Preventing automated systems from indexing private communications for state or corporate oversight.

Verifiability

Requiring all AI training models to provide clear proof of affirmative, voluntary user opt-in before data ingestion.

The Efficiency Argument

Expert analysis confirms that data integrity increases when information is provided voluntarily. By focusing on explicit consent, we ensure that the AI used in critical sectors like medicine and security is more accurate, more ethical, and more reliable.
